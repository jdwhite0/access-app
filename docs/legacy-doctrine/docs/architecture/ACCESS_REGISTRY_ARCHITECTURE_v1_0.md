# ACCESS Registry Architecture
## Version 1.0

### Purpose

This document defines how ownership, identity, relationships, and connections are represented inside the ACCESS ecosystem.

This architecture serves as the foundation for future databases, APIs, applications, agents, and user experiences.

ACCESS is the ownership and orientation layer of the ecosystem.

---

# Core Principle

Everything important should be registerable.

If something creates value, ACCESS should be capable of tracking it, connecting it, and associating it with an owner.

---

# Registry Philosophy

The registry does not exist to store files.

The registry exists to establish ownership.

The registry exists to establish relationships.

The registry exists to establish context.

The registry exists to establish orientation.

---

# Registry Formula

Identity
↓
Owns
↓
Objects
↓
Connected Through
↓
Relationships

This is the foundational architecture of ACCESS.

---

# Primary Registry Object

Identity

Example:

jerry.access

Identity serves as the root ownership object.

Everything else ultimately connects back to an identity.

---

# Ownership Model

Identity can own:

- Organizations
- Projects
- Systems
- Assets
- Workflows
- Agents
- Blueprints
- Offers
- Vault Records
- Connections

Ownership is persistent.

Authentication methods may change.

Ownership remains.

---

# Organization Registry

Organizations may own:

- Systems
- Projects
- Assets
- Agents
- Workflows
- Knowledge

Example:

JD AI Systems
JD Productions
Forever Thriven

---

# World Registry

Worlds represent domains of participation.

Examples:

- Finance World
- Creator World
- Business World
- AI World
- Media World

Users may participate in multiple worlds simultaneously.

---

# System Registry

Systems represent repeatable structures.

Examples:

- Financial System
- Sales System
- Content System
- Hiring System

Systems may contain:

- Projects
- Assets
- Workflows
- Agents

---

# Project Registry

Projects represent temporary initiatives.

Projects may create:

- Assets
- Systems
- Offers
- Knowledge Records

Projects connect discovery to execution.

---

# Asset Registry

Assets represent future value.

Examples:

- Businesses
- Domains
- Websites
- Content
- Intellectual Property
- Automations

Assets may belong to:

- Individuals
- Organizations

---

# Workflow Registry

Workflows define repeatable processes.

Workflows may connect:

- Systems
- Projects
- Agents
- Assets

---

# Agent Registry

Agents represent intelligence systems.

Agents may:

- Observe
- Analyze
- Create
- Execute
- Recommend

Agents may participate in workflows and systems.

---

# Blueprint Registry

Blueprints represent structured designs.

Examples:

- Business Blueprint
- Offer Blueprint
- System Blueprint
- Automation Blueprint

Blueprints are created by JYSON and executed by Builder.

---

# Offer Registry

Offers represent value exchanges.

Examples:

- Service
- Product
- Membership
- Subscription
- License

Offers connect assets to revenue.

---

# Knowledge Registry

Knowledge records include:

- Research
- Notes
- Frameworks
- Lessons
- Strategies

Knowledge records are preserved by Vault.

---

# Connection Registry

Connections represent relationships between objects.

Examples:

Identity → Organization

Organization → System

System → Project

Project → Asset

Asset → Offer

Agent → Workflow

Connections are first-class objects.

Connections create leverage.

---

# Relationship Graph

The ecosystem should eventually function as a graph.

Objects are not isolated.

Objects are connected.

The graph allows users to understand:

- What exists
- What belongs to them
- What connects to what
- What opportunities exist

---

JYSON

---

---

---

---

# Long-Term Destination

The ACCESS Registry becomes the ownership graph of the ecosystem.

Users should eventually be able to see:

- Their identity
- Their organizations
- Their systems
- Their projects
- Their assets
- Their workflows
- Their agents
- Their knowledge
- Their connections

from a single place.

---

# Final Principle

The registry is not a database.

The registry is a living map of ownership, relationships, and value.

ACCESS exists to help users understand where they are, what they own, and how everything connects.
